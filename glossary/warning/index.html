Glossary / Content Warnings

⚠️ Content Warnings

74 terms in this category · 47 with detailed pages + 27 filterable tags.

Ableism
Depictions of discrimination, prejudice, or harmful attitudes toward disabled individuals.
Abuse
Physical, emotional, psychological, verbal, or sexual abuse depicted within the story.
Abuse Between Mcs
Abuse occurs between the main romantic characters. A serious flag many readers want to know about going in.
Alcoholism
Alcohol dependency or addiction is depicted.
Animal Abuse
Animals are intentionally harmed, neglected, or mistreated on-page.
Animal Death
The death of an animal is depicted or discussed.
Birth Control Non-Consent
One character interferes with, removes, or deceives another regarding birth control.
Body Betrayal
A character experiences unwanted physical attraction toward someone they dislike or distrust.
Child Death
The death of a child is depicted or discussed.
Death Grief
Significant death, mourning, or bereavement is central to the story.
Drug Abuse
Misuse or addiction involving drugs is depicted.
Dubious Consent
Sexual situations where consent is unclear, impaired, pressured, or ethically questionable.
Eating Disorders
Depictions of disordered eating behaviors or eating disorder recovery.
Fatphobia
Depictions of anti-fat bias, discrimination, or body shaming.
Forced Pregnancy
Pregnancy occurs through coercion, manipulation, magical compulsion, or lack of consent.
Gambling
Problematic or significant gambling behavior is present.
Graphic Violence
Violence depicted in explicit detail — gore, torture, brutal combat.
Human Trafficking
Characters experience or are involved in forced exploitation or trafficking.
Incest
Sexual or romantic relationships between close family members.
Infertility
Difficulty conceiving or carrying a pregnancy plays a role in the story.
Mental Illness
Characters experience diagnosed or implied mental health conditions — depression, anxiety, psychosis, etc.
Mental Trauma
Characters experience significant psychological trauma — flashbacks, PTSD, dissociation.
Miscarriage
Pregnancy loss is depicted or discussed.
Misogyny
Sexism, discrimination, or prejudice against women appears in the story.
No Hea
The story does not conclude with a Happily Ever After. Important to flag — many romance readers consider HEA a genre requirement.
Non-Consent Between MCs
Sexual activity occurs between main characters without consent.
Non-Traditional HEA
The story ends positively but the ending differs from a conventional romantic resolution.
Past Abuse
A character has a history of abuse predating the events of the story.
Past Child Abuse
References to abuse experienced during childhood — character backstory.
Past Child Neglect
References to neglect experienced during childhood — backstory or memory.
Past Sexual Abuse
A character has a history of sexual abuse before the story begins.
Queerphobia
Homophobia, transphobia, or discrimination against LGBTQ+ individuals appears in the story.
Racism
Racial prejudice, discrimination, or racial violence appears in the story.
Rape
Sexual assault or rape is depicted or discussed. Always flag — readers deserve advance notice.
Religious Trauma
Psychological harm resulting from religious beliefs, institutions, or experiences.
Self-Harm
Intentional self-injury is depicted or discussed.
Slavery
Slavery exists or plays a major role in the story.
Slut Shaming
Characters are criticized, judged, or humiliated for sexual behavior.
Stalking
Persistent unwanted surveillance, following, or monitoring of a character.
Substance Abuse
Problematic use of drugs, alcohol, or other substances.
Suicide
Suicide attempts, suicide, or suicidal ideation are depicted or discussed.
Terminal Illness
A character suffers from a life-limiting or incurable illness.
Third Party Abuse
Abuse committed by a character outside the primary romantic relationship.
Third-Party Sexual Abuse
Sexual assault committed by someone outside the primary relationship.
Torture Of Mcs
A main character experiences torture.
Torture Of Side Characters
A secondary character experiences torture.
Victim Blaming
Responsibility for abuse, assault, or harm is placed on the victim rather than the perpetrator.
