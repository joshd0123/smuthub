The romantasy encyclopedia.

Romantasy is overwhelming — tropes, moods, content warnings, abbreviations, reading orders, BookTok shorthand. This is your decoder. Every term connects to real books you can shelf, so you don''t just learn what a "shadow daddy" is — you find five of them.

⚔️Tropes75

Abduction
A character is taken — often by the love interest, sometimes by an antagonist.
Age Gap
A significant age difference between the romantic leads — experience meets youth, or vice versa.
Arranged Marriage
Characters married off by family, court, or contract — love arrives second.
Best Friend's Brother
A romance with your best friend's sibling — forbidden, awkward, irresistible.
Best Friend's Parent
Romance with a best friend's parent. Forbidden, complicated, the kind of trope readers know exactly why they're here for.
Betrayal
A major breach of trust drives the story — secrets revealed, sides switched, love tested.
Boss / Employee
A romance between a superior and subordinate — workplace ethics, complicated power, very motivated readers.
Caretaking
One character actively looks after another — illness, injury, emotional crisis — and the line between caretaker and lover blurs.
Cheating
Infidelity occurs within a romantic relationship — sometimes the inciting incident, sometimes the wound.
Chosen One
A character marked by prophecy, magic, or fate for an important role.
Court Intrigue
Political scheming, shifting alliances, and betrayals inside a royal or fae court.
Dark Romance
A subgenre and trope where the romance involves morally compromised characters, dubious-consent dynamics, violence, captor relationships, or other content most romance shies away from.
Deadly Trials
Characters must survive lethal tests, competitions, or challenges.
Dragon Rider
A trope where the male love interest is a dragon rider — usually in an academy or military setting, with a soul-bonded dragon and a high-stakes magical war as the backdrop.
Enemies to Lovers
A romance in which the love interests start as antagonists — rivals, opposing sides, mutual loathing — and gradually convert that animosity into desire.
Enemy Kingdoms
The leads belong to warring nations, courts, or factions.
Fae
Magical beings associated with fairy lore — tall, often pointed-eared, otherworldly in beauty, frequently organized into magical courts with their own powers and politics.
Fake Dating
Characters pretend to be a couple — for a wedding, a contract, family pressure — and discover the pretense is concealing real feelings.
Fake Engagement
Characters pretend to be engaged for personal, social, or strategic reasons — until pretend gets too real.
Fallen Angel
An angelic being cast out from heaven or stripped of divine status — a different kind of grace.
Fated Mates
A trope where two characters are magically, spiritually, or cosmically destined to be together — often with consequences for rejecting the bond.
Fish Out of Water
A character dropped into an unfamiliar world, court, or culture, learning the rules in real time.
Forced Proximity
A trope where characters who would otherwise stay apart are required by circumstance to be together — sharing a cabin, road trip, safehouse, or single room.
Found Family
Characters form deep family-like bonds outside of blood — chosen, fought for, protected.
Friends to Lovers
A romance that starts in established friendship and transitions into something more.
Friends with Benefits
Sex without commitment between people who like each other — until one of them stops being able to keep it casual.
Good Grovel
A character who caused harm or betrayal makes a meaningful, satisfying effort to earn forgiveness — not a quick "sorry."
Grumpy x Sunshine
A pairing of one perpetually scowling character and one relentlessly warm one — the grump goes soft only for the sunshine.
He Falls First
A pacing trope where the male love interest realizes his feelings well before the heroine does.
High Lord
A powerful ruler overseeing a magical territory, kingdom, or court — used to being obeyed; undone only by her.
Hurt / Comfort
One character experiences pain — physical or emotional — and another provides care and presence.
Instalove
Characters fall deeply in love almost immediately after meeting.
Love Triangle
Romantic conflict involving three people — and only one can win.
Mafia Romance
Romance set in organized crime — loyalty, danger, blood oaths, complicated love.
Marriage of Convenience
Characters marry for strategy, safety, or politics — and fall in love after the paperwork.
Military Romance
Romance involving active military service or veterans — duty, deployment, coming home.
Monster Romance
A romance with a non-human or monstrous love interest.
Morally Grey
A character whose ethics are flexible — capable of cruelty, manipulation, or violence, usually with a justification that makes the reader complicit.
Multicultural Romance
A romance involving characters from different cultural backgrounds — bridging, blending, sometimes clashing.
Mutual Pining
Both characters are in love with each other but neither has said it — sometimes neither has admitted it to themselves.
Only One Bed
A setup where the characters must share a single sleeping space — by reservation error, snowstorm, hideout, or contrived circumstance.
Parent's Best Friend
Romance with a parent's close friend — they've known you since you were small. Now they're seeing you differently.
Pregnancy
A pregnancy plays a central role in the plot or relationship.
Quest Romance
A romance that ignites on a dangerous journey or mission.
Redemption Arc
A character works to atone for past wrongs and become someone better.
Rejected Mate
A fated bond that one partner refuses, at least at first — the rejection itself becomes the wound the story heals.
Rekindled Romance
Former partners who find their way back to each other after time apart.
Revenge
A story driven by a character's pursuit of vengeance.
Reverse Love Triangle
Multiple characters pursue the same romantic interest — the protagonist as the prize.
Road Trip
Romance that develops while characters travel together.
Royal Romance
Love among kings, queens, princes, and the thrones that complicate them.
Second Chance
Former romantic partners reconnect and attempt to rebuild their relationship.
Secret Baby
A character discovers they have a child they were previously unaware of.
Secret Relationship
Characters hide their romantic involvement from others — family, court, public.
Secret Royalty
A character who unknowingly holds a claim to a throne, title, or power.
Shadow Daddy
A hero archetype: intimidating, dark, often morally grey, with shadow- or void-based magical abilities; possessive and devoted to his love interest.
She Fell Harder
A companion pacing trope: when "he fell first" lands, her eventual feelings arrive with more force.
Shifter
Romance featuring characters who shift between human and animal forms — wolves and dragons most common, but anything from big cats to bears. Often includes pack dynamics and mate bonds.
Sibling's Best Friend
A romance with your sibling's close friend — knew them forever, never like this.
Slow Burn
A romance where attraction builds over a long stretch of the book — sometimes the whole book — with delayed physical resolution.
Snowed In
Characters trapped together by weather — forced proximity with a fireplace.
Soul Bond
A character who is biologically or magically destined to be the partner of another — without the lifelong commitment implication of "mate."
Soulmates
Two characters magically or spiritually bonded — sometimes telepathically, sometimes through ritual, sometimes by birth.
Step-Siblings
A romance between characters who become siblings through marriage — not blood.
Superhero Romance
Romance featuring characters with superpowers or hidden identities.
Survival
Characters must endure dangerous conditions to survive — isolation forges intimacy.
Time-Travel Romance
Lovers separated or united across different eras.
Touch Her and Die
A possessive hero archetype where the love interest's extreme protectiveness becomes a threat to anyone who looks sideways at the heroine.
Tournament
Competitors face each other in a structured magical or martial contest.
Training Arc
A stretch where the protagonist is forged through structured training and earns their power.
True Mate
A shifter/paranormal variation of fated mates — the destined partner, often recognized by scent or the shifter's animal side, sometimes distinguished from a "chosen mate" picked by logic rather than fate.
Unrequited Love
One character carries romantic feelings the other doesn't return — at least not yet.
Villain Romance
A romance where the love interest is the story's antagonist — or close to it.
Virgin Hero
A male love interest who hasn't had sex before the events of the book.
Wartime Romance
Love that takes root during active conflict.

Romance set in the present-day real world — no magic, no time travel, just modern people falling in love.

A romance subgenre that leans into morally compromised characters, dubious or non-consent dynamics, violence, and content that pushes past the comfort zone of mainstream romance.
Demon Romance
A romance featuring a demon or demonic love interest.
Dystopian Romance
Romance set in a broken, oppressive, or post-collapse society.
Fantasy
A story featuring magical or supernatural elements as central to the world or plot.
Funny / Romantic Comedy
Romance with humor as a primary tone — banter, hijinks, situational comedy.
High Fantasy
Fantasy set in a fully fictional secondary world with extensive worldbuilding, often featuring epic stakes and elaborate magic systems.
Historical Romance
Romance set in the past — Regency, Victorian, medieval, or any historical period.
Horror Romance
Romance with horror elements designed to unsettle as much as seduce.
Paranormal Romance
Romance with supernatural elements — vampires, shifters, witches, fae, demons, ghosts.
Pirate Romance
A romance set among pirates, privateers, or seafaring adventurers.
Romantasy
A genre that blends fantasy and romance — magical worlds and significant worldbuilding with a central love story driving the plot.
Romantic Mystery
Romance interwoven with a mystery the leads must solve together.
Romantic Suspense
Romance built around tension, danger, and uncertainty driving the plot.
Sports Romance

Urban Fantasy

Witch Romance
A romance centered on a witch or magical practitioner.

Affinity
The specific type of magic a character can wield — elemental, mind-based, or esoteric.
Blood Magic
Magic that requires blood as a source — costly, intimate, often forbidden.
Blood Oath
A promise sealed in blood and enforced by magic.
Cursed Objects
Magical items carrying dangerous effects — heirlooms, weapons, jewelry with a price.
Dark Magic
Magic tied to death, corruption, sacrifice, or forbidden power.
Dragon Bond
The magical, telepathic tether between a rider and their dragon.
Dragon Riders
Humans bonded to dragons — telepathic link, war partner, soul match.
Elemental Magic
Magic powered by control of natural elements — fire, water, earth, air.
Gods & Goddesses
Divine beings directly influence events — favored mortals, divine bargains, prophecy.
Kingdoms & Empires
Large political powers drive the story — alliances, wars, succession.
Magic System
The rules governing how power works in a given world — its sources, costs, and limits.
Magic System
The structured set of rules governing how magic works in a world — sources, limits, costs.
Magical Academy
A school dedicated to teaching magic — Fourth Wing, ACOTAR's Night Court training, Hogwarts-adjacent.
Magical Creatures
Supernatural beings populate the world — dragons, griffins, fae beasts.
Mate Bond
The supernatural tie that marks two people as partners — by scent, fate, or magic.
Necromancy
Magic of death, spirits, and resurrection — taboo in most worlds, devastating where allowed.
Parallel Worlds
Multiple realities or dimensions exist — what-ifs given form.
Portal Key
A magic-infused key that transports the bearer to a chosen place — often single-use, often pricey.
Portals
Characters travel between worlds or realms via doorways, mirrors, or magical thresholds.
Prophecy
Future events are foretold and shape the plot — the chosen one, the inevitable, the loophole.
Reincarnation
Characters reborn across lifetimes — lovers finding each other again and again.
Royal Courts
Political systems ruled by nobility — succession, alliances, scheming nobles.
Scry
Divination using crystals, water, blood, or tokens to find people or learn secrets.

🌶️Spice & Door13

Behind Closed Doors
Intimate scenes occur off-page — the camera cuts away at the bedroom door.
Burn
A descriptor for how fast a romance develops sexually — distinct from "slow-burn" the trope. A book can be "fast-burn" (immediate heat) or "slow-burn" (delayed).
Closed Door
Romance where sex happens but isn't shown — fade-to-black, morning-after framing, imagination filling the gap.
D/s (Dominance / Submission)
A power-exchange dynamic — bedroom or 24/7 — where one partner takes control and one yields. Consensual is the only kind that counts.
Explicit & Plentiful
Frequent, highly explicit intimate scenes. The spiciest tier.
Explicit Open Door
Intimate scenes shown on the page with explicit detail.
Fade to Black
A scene that cuts away just before or during a sexual encounter — the close-door convention in action.
Glimpses & Kisses
Minimal on-page intimacy — hand-holding, embraces, the occasional kiss. Sweetest end of the spice scale.
High Spice
Romance with very explicit sexual content — typically 4–5 chilis on smutHub's scale.
Open Door
Romance where sex scenes are shown explicitly on the page.
Safe Word
An agreed-upon word that immediately stops a scene in kink contexts. Non-negotiable infrastructure for consent.
Smut
Affectionate shorthand for explicit sexual content in romance.
Spice (Heat Level)
A reader-shorthand rating for how explicit a book's sexual content is, usually on a 0–5 chili-pepper scale.

Series
Multiple connected books, either one continuous story or interconnected characters in a shared world.
Standalone
A complete story in a single book — no cliffhanger, no required reading after it.

👁️POV5

Dual POV
A story alternates between two characters' perspectives — usually the main love interests.
First-Person POV
Narrated as "I felt," "I knew" — placing the reader fully inside one character.
POV (Point of View)
The character whose perspective the reader experiences. Romantasy is largely written in 1st-person POV; multi-POV is common in epic romantasy.
Single POV
The story is told from one character's perspective — usually the protagonist.
Third-Person POV
Narrated as "she did," "he knew" — a step outside the character with more flexibility to show multiple perspectives.

🐺Omegaverse9

Alpha
In omegaverse: the designation that experiences ruts, knots, and often projects dominance. Personality varies — not all alphas are alphaholes.
Heat
In omegaverse: a biological cycle where Omegas experience intense drive to mate. Usually requires an Alpha or suppressants to manage. Distinct from "heat level" (spice rating).
Knotting
A canine/wolf-derived mating mechanic in omegaverse and shifter romance where the Alpha "knots" during sex, physically bonding the pair.
Mpreg
Male pregnancy — common in omegaverse where male Omegas can carry young. A whole subgenre with dedicated readers.
Omega
In omegaverse: the designation that experiences heats, can be knotted, often bears young regardless of gender. Not inherently submissive — personality varies.
Omegaverse
A romance worldbuilding system featuring biological designations — Alphas (rut), Omegas (heat), Betas (neutral) — with knotting, scent-bonding, mate dynamics, and often mpreg. Distinct from basic shifter pack romance though they often overlap.
Scent Marking
In shifter and omegaverse romance: leaving your scent on a mate to claim them. Ranges from subtle (a press of the wrist) to primal.
Shifting
The transformation between human and animal forms. Painful or seamless depending on the world. Some can partial-shift (claws, eyes). Often tied to emotion, the moon, or choice.
Suppressants
In omegaverse: medication that suppresses heats or ruts. Lets characters function "normally" but often has plot-relevant side effects or failures.
